# Roadmap

## Current Focus

This repository currently explores how experienced operational judgment can be captured, refined, and reused.

The core ideas are:

- Operational Knowledge
- Context Engineering
- Operational Memory
- Human Feedback Loops
- Deployment Intelligence
- Operational Judgment

## Current Thesis

Operational Intelligence is not simply collecting more telemetry.

Operational Intelligence is the practice of combining data, context, experience, and judgment to help people make better operational decisions.

## Future Exploration

### Confidence Models

How should an operational intelligence system represent confidence?

### Hypothesis-Driven Analysis

How can systems generate multiple possible explanations instead of jumping to one conclusion?

### Contradictory Evidence

How can systems intentionally search for evidence that challenges their own assessment?

### Observability for AI-Assisted Operations

How do metrics, logs, traces, and operational context help AI-assisted systems reason more effectively?

### Operational Trust

How do engineers develop trust in systems that assist with operational decisions?

### Safe Autonomy

What kinds of operational actions are safe to automate, and what should always require human review?

## Final Thought

The long-term goal is not more automation.

The long-term goal is better operational decision making.
